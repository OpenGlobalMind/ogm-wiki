The Future isn't AI. It’s CHI.

(draft) Collective Hybrid Intelligence.

We are all feeling the ground shift.

GenAI is dissolving the "soft tissue" of our daily work — the emails, the summaries, the reports, the basic code — at breathtaking speed.

For many, this creates a quiet terror. I call it the Sword of Damocles Effect: If the software does 80% of my tasks, will my employer keep me for the remaining 20%? When will the sword drop on me?

That fear is the single biggest blocker to innovation and transformation today. It freezes us.

But what if we flipped the script?

I’ve been a "Cyborg" for 28 years.

Long before ChatGPT, I began melding my mind with software called TheBrain. I didn't use it to replace my thinking; I used it to extend it.

When you truly partner with software—when you move from "user" to "Cyborg"—you don't get replaced. You get Advanced Chess: A human+machine team that outperforms any AI (or human) alone.

From AI to CHI

The goal shouldn't be Artificial Intelligence. It should be CHI: Collective Hybrid Intelligence.

As an Aikido practitioner, I love that "CHI" echoes Ki—the flow of energy.

  • AI is a tool that crunches data.

  • CHI is a flow of intelligence between humans and machines, orchestrated by trust.

The "Design from Trust" Key

You cannot build CHI in a culture of surveillance. You can't ask employees to meld with a system that holds a sword over their heads.

To win this future, we have to move from Design from Mistrust (monitoring inputs) to Design from Trust (unleashing outcomes).

I’m developing a new talk on how to make this shift—how to stop fearing the "task dissolution" and start building a Collective Intelligence that is wiser, faster, and more human than anything we’ve seen before.

Are you ready to build the Cyborg future?
