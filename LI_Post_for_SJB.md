_When did you last have a conversation that actually changed how you think?_ Everyone's outsourcing that to AI. I'm doing the opposite.

For 28 years I've been building an external brain — over half a million connected nodes, curated by hand, open to the public. Not a corpus. Not a dataset. A living map of how one curious human has connected ideas across nearly three decades. It's called [Jerry's Brain](https://jerrysbrain.com/) (built in [TheBrain](https://thebrain.com/) software), and it includes everything from [trust models](https://bra.in/4jY6RA) and [useful reframes](https://bra.in/8px2RK) to [Angine de Poitrine](https://bra.in/6vALE3) and the [lovefest around Agentic AI](https://bra.in/6jkeaZ).

What's remarkable isn't the Brain itself, it's what happens when a room full of people throw live questions at it.

I navigate in real time, narrating as I go. But here's what surprises people: it's not just me, and it's not just the Brain — it's the collision. Twenty-eight years of curation, my live synthesis, and _your_ room's actual questions, all hitting at once. A question about the future of product marketing reminds me of a node about the [history of potatoes](https://bra.in/5vPoaJ) that nobody saw coming. The audience's question determines the destination, but the journey reveals connections none of us planned. That's not a presentation. That's live sensemaking, together.

I've turned this into a live session format for virtual events: [Stump Jerry's Brains](https://stumpjerrysbrains.com/). SJB is part knowledge art, part escape room, part Red Team for your beliefs.

No prepared arc. No net. Not a polished deck. Entirely bespoke and unrepeatable.
