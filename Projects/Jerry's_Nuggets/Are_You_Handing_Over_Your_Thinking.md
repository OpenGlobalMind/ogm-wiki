# Are You Handing Over Your Thinking?
 (draft) 
*Subhed.* 

Your organization is deciding right now, mostly by accident, how much of its thinking to outsource.

Not in a strategy meeting. In a thousand small moments: the meeting summary nobody checks against what was actually said. The first draft that quietly becomes the final draft. The question nobody asks a colleague anymore, because an answer arrives in three seconds — smooth, confident, and unsigned.

None of these moments feels like a decision. That's exactly what makes them decisions of the most consequential kind.

I've spent thirty years on the other side of that line, building an external memory of my own — 644,000 ideas, over a million links, every one of them a choice I made and can stand behind. From over here, the boundary between human thinking and machine thinking doesn't look scary. It looks like the most interesting place in the world to stand.

More on this soon. Meanwhile, I'm curious: where have you watched your organization's thinking quietly change hands?
