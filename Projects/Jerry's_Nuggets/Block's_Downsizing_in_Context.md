The recent news that Block (the company that owns Square and Afterpay) is cutting its workforce from over 10,000 to under 6,000 caused a stir. You can read CEO Jack Dorsey's note to staff [here](https://x.com/jack/status/2027129697092731343). 

Even though Block may have been suffering from Pandemic bloat and other non-AI-related issues, the news washed over the business world as, "omg, if everyone cuts 40% of their staff because of AI...."

This post is a frame for several subsequent posts, which will tackle parts of this puzzle in turn. They are, in rough sequence:

- In today's low-trust workplace, Generative AI is a Sword of Damocles hanging over everyone's head. When will the thread snap, and my job be eliminated? 
- GenAI doesn't replace jobs (yet), but it does wipe out tasks. 
- Knock enough tasks of your job description and you and your employer better be flexible! 
- And if your job could mostly be automated, is your *career* doomed? 
- How can you train up for a new career before GenAI automates your new field?
